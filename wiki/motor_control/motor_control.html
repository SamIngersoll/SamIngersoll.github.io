Motor Control

Commutation is the process of switching the phase or direction of current to produce motion in a motor.

Cogging is torque due to the interactions between the permanent magnets (PM) and the stator in PM machines. It feels like the rotor is skipping or jumping as it rotates past each pole pair. Cogging can be characterized in one electric revolution and can be at least partially compensated for in the controller.

Basic Control (open loop)

The most simple commutation strategy for a BLDC motor would be to provide a predefined waveform to the coils and have them pull the rotor up to speed. This open loop strategy is not a good way to run a BLDC motor because it power inefficient (especially at higher speeds) and does not drive the rotor smoothly. Both of these drawbacks result from the fact that the motor is commutated without consideration of rotor position, this can result in the magnetic field inadvertantly pushing the rotor in the incorrect direction if the commutation happens at the wrong time.
