Motors/Motor Control

Selecting a motor

Torque (\(\tau\)) is proportional to the height of the motor and the radius squared, this is why pancake motors are more torque dense than machines of other shapes.

Many motor vendors dont list the torque that a motor can produce. Motors of similar design will have a similar torque density \( \tau_d\) \[ \tau_d = \frac{\tau}{A_{ag} \cdot r_{ag}}\] Where \( A_{ag} \) is the surface area at the airgap and \( r_{ag} \) is the radius at the airgap. \( \tau_d\) can be used to approximate the torque of a given motor based on the known torque of a motor of similar design.

An 8608 pancake motor that can produce a peak torque of approximately 3Nm would have a \( \tau_d \) equal to \[ \tau_d = \frac{3Nm}{\pi \cdot 0.86m \cdot 0.08m \cdot 0.43m} = 135.2 \frac{Nm}{m^3} \] We could then multiply \(\tau_d\) by \(A_{ag} \cdot r_{ag}\) of a new motor to get a peak torque estimate for a new motor.


Voltage constant (\(K_V\)) describes how the motor spins in relation to voltage provided, it has the units \(\frac{rpm}{V}\).
Torque constant (\(K_T\)) describes how much torque the motor produces per amp of current provided, it has the units \(\frac{Nm}{amp}\).

The voltage and torque constant are related as follows \[ K_T = \frac{60}{2\pi K_V} \] Above we converted \(\frac{rpm}{V}\) to \( \frac{rad}{s \cdot V} \) and took the reciprocal of \(K_V\).
\(K_V \cdot K_T = 1 \)

Intuition

Normally voltage is thought of as producing force and current is thought of as producing velocity, in a motor, the opposite is true. In an electric motor, current produces torque and voltage produces rpm.

Architectures and applications

Gimball motors have to be smooth, so they generally include higher quality parts (like bearings), they also have low \(K_V\) becuase they are meant to spin slowly.

RC vehicle motors (generally quadcopters and helicopters) are usually designed to have high voltage constants in order to get more rpm and more lift.

Mechanical Design

Fusing

Mechanical fusing: when one part of an assembly is specifically designed to fail first and at a known load. Mechanical fusing can be used to ensure that cheap, easily replaceable parts fail before expensive or hard to replace parts.

Clutching: is a nondestructive form of mechanical fusing where at a known load, slipping occurs to prevent failure. When the load is reduced below the threshold the slipping stops.

Fabrication

Tolerances

3D printer: holes will shrink in diameter by 0.01in. Leave an additional 0.005in for a good press fit.
