First Order Linear Differential Equations

The solution to a differential equation is a function or class of functions.

Homogeneous Differential Equataions

Come in the form \( \dot{y} + p(t)y = 0 \)

Inhomogeneous Differential Equations

Come in the form \( \dot{y} + p(t)y = q(t) \)

Solving First Order ODEs

There are many ways to solve first order linear differential equations. These are listed in order of how they should be attempted when approaching a problem.

Separation of variables

(For homogeneous ODEs)
Separation of variables is the first method that should be attempted when approaching a problem because it is the easiest and leads to a quick and clean solution.
  1. Move all \(y\) functions to one side and functions of \(t\) to the other side.
  2. Integrate both sides (they should both be functions of a single variable, \(y\) or \(t\)).
  3. (If given) Plug in initial condition to solve for integrating constant and find unique solution.
\[ \dot{y} + p(t)y = 0 \] \[ \dot{y} = -p(t)y \] \[ \frac{\dot{y}}{y} = -p(t) \] \[ \int \frac{1}{y}dy = \int p(t) dt \] \[ ln(y) = P(t)+C \] \[ y = e^{P(t)+C} \] \[ y = e^{P(t)}e^C \] \[ y = Ce^{P(t)} \]

Superposition

(For inhomogeneous ODEs)
If the general solution to the homogeneous differential equation can be found as well as a particular solution to the inhomogeneous equation, the two can be added together to get the general solution to the inhomogeneous differential equation.
